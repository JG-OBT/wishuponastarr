Cookie Policy

Plain-English Cookie Transparency

1. What Cookies Are

Cookies are simple, small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device by websites that you visit. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide structural information to the owners of the site.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to improve your user experience on our website. This includes storing your chosen layout preferences (such as confirming whether you have accepted optional cookies so we do not prompt you on every single page load).

3. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are vital for the fundamental operation of the website and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms.

Cookie Name Provider Purpose Duration
wuas_cookie_consent Wish Upon a Starre Stores your cookie consent preferences (Strictly Necessary, Analytics, Marketing). 1 Year

4. Optional Analytics Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore completely anonymous.

Cookie Name Provider Purpose Duration
_ga, _gid Google Analytics (Optional) Anonymously tracks visitor patterns, device types, and page loading speeds. 2 Years / 24 Hours

Note: These cookies are turned off by default and will never be placed on your device unless you actively click "Accept optional cookies" or toggle "Analytics" to ON inside our preferences modal.

5. Third-Party Cookies & Content

We may occasionally embed video players (e.g. YouTube or Vimeo) or social media feeds on our site to show footage of our characters performing at events. These external third-party providers may set their own tracking cookies when you click play on their content. We do not control these third-party trackers.
